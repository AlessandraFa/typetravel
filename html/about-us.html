The purpose of this web site is to explore various types of typographic and layout style for text documents, as an end-of-course project for the "Information Modelling and Web Technologies" course of the Master Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge of the University of Bologna, held by professor Fabio Vitali.

The documents contained in this web site have been selected for their length and complexity from various websites and magazines. Their publication here is not intended to be an alternative or replace their original locations.You can find the original publications at:

  • Architect Magazine: The Climate Is Changing. So Must Architecture
  • The Guardian: Concrete: the most destructive material on Earth
  • Longreads: Building In the Shadow of Our Own Destruction
  • Medium: Tracking Historical Representations of the Female Nude
  • BBC: The fine line between art and pornography
  • Artsy: The Taboo History of Women’s Body Hair in Art

Meet our team

Edoardo Dal Borgo

I am a graduate in philosophy from the University of Bologna, and I am currently attending the master's degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge at the University of Bologna. My studies are now focused on data analysis, machine learning and knowledge extraction. I also work as a project manager and digital marketing strategist for my digital communication agency. For this project, I have curated the user experience design of the whole platform, with particular attention to usability aspects, responsive design, and the navigation process. Starting from the UX design, I developed the Bootstrap 5 layout structure for the whole website. I edited the issue "Female nude painting throughout Art", I developed the text markup of the articles of my issue, and I designed the data structure of the metadata contained in the articles of all the issues of the website. Then I extracted all these data and developed the markup of the section of the two issues that allows browsing inside the articles' metadata. I also am also the designer of the algorithmic part of the project, and I have developed all the JavaScript scripts, in particular: the script that allows users to change the style of the website pages and the script that allows users to browse articles of the issues by clicking on the metadata collected in the specific section of the issues pages.

Alessandra Failla

I am a graduate in European Art History and Political Economics from the University of Heidelberg, and I am currently attending the Master Degree Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge at the University of Bologna. My main interest are in web design and in history of design. I am also interested in the analysis and management of cultural heritage, especially in visual art digitalisation. I work for the Kunsthistorisches Institut in Florenz as a consultant researcher. My main responsibilities involve research and cataloguing of historical photographs within a Europe-wide database. My main contribution to this project was the layout design. I developed the design of all themes, including the necessary research in each historical period. The main areas of work involved: researching of fonts and images that were approriate to the periods of interest; editing of images and creation of animations; choosing layouts and color schemes; writing the documentation with all relevant choices; finally, I worked on the markup of the issue "The future of architecture".
